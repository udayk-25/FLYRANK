Search Intelligence: Prioritizing Content Refresh Opportunities

Python Random Forest DuckDB Pandas Precision@50

01. The Problem

Enterprise websites lose massive search traffic to silent content decay because editorial teams waste hundreds of hours manually auditing healthy or low-impact pages. Heuristic rules (such as "flag any page older than 180 days") suffer from high false-positive rates, exhausting team capacity.

02. Engineering Decisions

Using Python, Pandas, and DuckDB, I queried 30,000 Search Console and Analytics rows. Instead of training a Random Forest classifier to maximize general accuracy across the entire long tail, I aligned the model's loss function with real-world editor capacity by optimizing for Precision@50 (the 50 pages an editorial team can actually refresh per week).

I deliberately excluded target proxy leakage fields (`trend_pct` and `trend_direction`) to ensure that all features (`impressions_90d`, `ctr`, `avg_position`, `days_since_last_update`) were knowable prior to prediction.

03. The Outcome & Metrics

The Random Forest model achieved a 74.0% Precision@50 score, representing a 3x lift over baseline rules. Out of the 50 pages prioritized each week, 37 are verified decaying pages requiring immediate refresh, saving 15+ editorial audit hours weekly.
